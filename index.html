Software Developer in training

For a long time, I've tried to figure out the direction I want my life to take. A desire for financial independence has always been strong, but I really wanted to do something I enjoy. My brother-in-law, who is a front end developer, suggested I try some free coding websites to see if I like it. At first, I thought anyone who did this for a living was clinically insane and possibly part robot.
Then, I started understanding.
When I was able to figure out a bug on my own, or type in a few characters and create an action in a separate environment, I was a goner. I immediately started comparing schooling options, and after a short while, I felt comfortable commiting to Digital Crafts. In August, my children will be going to school full time (barring another pandemic) and I've decided to become a developer. As the term developer has many different paths, I'll have to narrow down an exact avenue, but until then I'm thoroughly enjoying learning it all. I feel invincible every time I solve an issue with my code.

This is what I need to accomplish my goal:

  1. Go to Digital Crafts Web Development Boot Camp.
  2. Work extremely hard to learn anything and everything I can.
  3. Build beautiful projects with drop-dead gorgeous code to go in my portfolio.
  4. Network and fully immerse myself in the IT world and build friendships with wonderful people that will help me grow and learn.
  5. Get a job as a developer at a company with good, strong ethics.
  6. Profit!
